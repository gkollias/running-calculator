üìö Running Training Guide

Understanding VDOT

VDOT is a measure of your current running fitness based on your race performance. Developed by exercise physiologist Jack Daniels, it provides a single number that represents your running ability and helps determine appropriate training paces.

Training Pace Guidelines

  • Easy Pace: 65-79% of max heart rate. Used for recovery runs and base building.
  • Marathon Pace: The pace you can sustain for 26.2 miles. Important for marathon training.
  • Threshold Pace: The pace you can sustain for about 1 hour. Improves lactate clearance.
  • Interval Pace: VO2 max pace. Improves maximum oxygen uptake.
  • Repetition Pace: Faster than 5K pace. Improves neuromuscular power and speed.

How to Use These Calculators

Start with the VDOT calculator using a recent race time to establish your current fitness level. Use the resulting training paces for your workouts. The pace calculator helps with daily run planning, while the split calculator is essential for race strategy. Monitor your heart rate zones during training to ensure you're hitting the right intensities.

How accurate are these running calculators?

Our calculators use scientifically validated formulas including Jack Daniels' VDOT equation and Riegel's formula for race predictions. While individual results may vary, these provide excellent estimates for training and racing purposes.

How often should I recalculate my VDOT?

Update your VDOT every 4-6 weeks or after significant races. As your fitness improves, your VDOT will increase, requiring adjustments to your training paces.
