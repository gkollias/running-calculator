Understanding VO2 Max

What is VO2 Max?

VO2 max (maximum oxygen uptake) is the maximum amount of oxygen your body can utilize during intense exercise. It's measured in milliliters of oxygen consumed per kilogram of body weight per minute (ml/kg/min).

VO2 Max Classifications:

  • Elite Athletes: 70+ ml/kg/min (men), 60+ ml/kg/min (women)
  • Excellent: 55-70 ml/kg/min (men), 45-60 ml/kg/min (women)
  • Good: 45-55 ml/kg/min (men), 35-45 ml/kg/min (women)
  • Average: 35-45 ml/kg/min (men), 25-35 ml/kg/min (women)
  • Below Average: <35 ml/kg/min (men), <25 ml/kg/min (women)

How to Improve VO2 Max:

  • High-intensity interval training (HIIT)
  • Tempo runs at lactate threshold
  • Long aerobic base building
  • Consistent training over time
